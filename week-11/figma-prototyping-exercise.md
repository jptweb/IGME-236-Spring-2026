## What You're Building

A clickable mobile prototype in Figma that demonstrates four core interaction patterns:

1. **Page-level vertical scroll** — content scrolls while header/footer stay fixed
2. **Component-level horizontal scroll** — a row of cards you can swipe through
3. **Cross-page navigation** — tap icons to move between screens
4. **Modal overlay** — tap a button to pop up a dialog, dismiss it by clicking outside or hitting close

These are the building blocks of virtually every mobile app prototype. Once you know these four patterns, you can prototype almost anything.

## Tips

- **Use your phone to test.** Download the Figma app, tap **Mirror**, and select your frame. Your prototype shows up on your actual device — way more realistic than the desktop preview.
- **Name your frames.** "Page 1 - Home" and "Page 2 - Detail" are much easier to work with than "Frame 1" and "Frame 2" when you're setting up navigation.
- **Components save time.** If your header and footer appear on every page, make them components before duplicating pages. Edit once, update everywhere.
- **Don't overthink the visuals.** This is a wireframe-level prototype. Gray boxes and placeholder text are fine — you're learning the interaction patterns, not designing a polished UI.
