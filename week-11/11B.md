## Don't Jump Straight Into Figma for Your Project

The tutorial exercise is about learning the tool. Your group's Lo-Fi Prototype (due Mon Apr 13) is about designing a solution. Those are different things.

Resist the urge to open Figma and start dragging rectangles around for your project right away. You'll end up staring at a blank canvas. Instead, work through these steps with your group today:

### 1. Review and Prioritize Your Research

Look back at your User Research findings as a team. What pain points were confirmed? What new problems surfaced?

Pick the **3-5 most important tasks** to focus on. You can't prototype everything.

### 2. Map Your Proposed Task Flows

In Week 8 you mapped the current UX. Now map the **new** experience. How should the flow work after your redesign?

Same task flow format. Know how screens connect before you design them.

### 3. Sketch Key Screens

This is optional but **strongly suggested** before building in Figma or Axure.

Try having each group member sketch ideas for key screens with pen and paper (10-15 min). Then compare. You'll be surprised how different they are. Circle what works, combine the best parts.

### 4. (Optional) Paper Walkthrough

Lay out your sketches as screens. Have someone "tap" through the flow. You'll catch navigation problems before opening any software.

Photograph it and include it in your project documentation.

### 5. Build Your Digital Prototype

NOW open Figma (or Axure). You're translating sketches into wireframes, not staring at a blank canvas.

Keep it lo-fi: grayscale, simple shapes, labeled buttons. This is not the time for colors and fonts.
