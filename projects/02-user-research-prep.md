# Prototype Project: User Research Prep

Now that you have an initial project idea, it's time to start doing the groundwork. As we discussed in class (and as you've worked on so far!), there is a lot of preparation that goes into the design process before we begin prototyping anything. This assignment is another one of the early steps in that process.

You've already conducted a competitive analysis to understand existing solutions and identify gaps. You will also conduct some targeted background research to better understand the problem space, and you will begin preparing research tools that will help you gather insights from real users.

Specifically, you will investigate whether the problems your team identified are commonly experienced by others (are your hunches right or are you barking up the wrong tree?), and you will draft (1) an interview script and (2) a survey that can later be used to collect feedback from users.

It is up to your group to decide how to divide up this work, but the expectation is that everyone is involved in the initial discussions for all aspects of this assignment. You'll have some time in-class to at least start these discussions.

> **A word of caution on splitting up the work:** For the interview and survey questions in particular, resist the temptation to just have each team member write a couple of questions independently and drop them into a shared doc. Questions need to work *together* as a coherent script — they should flow naturally, build on each other, and avoid redundancy. Use your in-class time to discuss and draft questions as a group, or at minimum, review and revise each other's questions together before finalizing.

## 1. Background Research

> **Note:** Some in your team may have already done some of this for their Week 6 writeup if you are still working on the same topic. If so, you may reuse and refine that work. If not, briefly gather this information again (for your new topic) as part of this assignment.

Determine whether other people have actually experienced the problems your team has described. This research will help you validate whether the issues you identified are real and meaningful for users besides yourselves.

**Where to look:**
- Customer forums on the vendor's website
- Relevant Reddit communities
- Product reviews or app store feedback for this or similar products
- General web searches using targeted queries

**Your goal:** Identify whether these problems appear repeatedly across different users and contexts.

**Summarize your findings.** For each problem your team identified, briefly:
- Summarize what you found
- Discuss whether this appears to be a common issue
- Identify whether a particular group of users seems to encounter the problem more often than others

## 2. Draft the Interview Script

Create a set of **5–10 open-ended interview questions** designed to elicit detailed responses from your target users. Focus on understanding their needs, frustrations, and experiences related to the problem or the software you are redesigning.

**Include an introduction:** Begin your interview script with a short explanation of what the interview will be about and why the participant's feedback is valuable. This introduction should be brief, provide context about the project, and set expectations for the conversation.

**Tips for crafting interview questions:**
- Avoid yes/no questions
- Ask about specific user experiences
- Keep questions conversational and easy to understand

**Example questions:**
- "Can you describe your typical experience when ordering coffee in the morning?"
- "What frustrates you the most when using your music app to create playlists?"
- "How often do you face challenges finding songs in large playlists?"

## 3. Draft the Survey

Create a survey with **5–7 questions** designed to collect more structured or quantitative feedback. The survey is intentionally short so people don't get bored and leave. Use a mix of question types to capture different kinds of information that you think will be important to answering the key question — re-read the intro if you're not sure what that is!

**Possible survey question types:**
- **Multiple-choice:** "How often do you use a mobile app to order coffee?"
- **Likert scale:** "How satisfied are you with your current playlist management tools?" (Very Satisfied – Not Satisfied – etc.)
- **Open-ended:** "What do you find most challenging about managing playlists?"

You don't need to put these into a survey tool yet, but make sure the format of your questions would work in one. (You could get started putting them into a form now if you want, but you won't be submitting it for this assignment.)

## 4. Plan Your Research

Decide who you plan to interview and survey. Aim to gather about **5 interview responses** and at least **10 survey responses** from users who fit your target user group. How do you plan to make it happen?

These should be users who are affected by the problem or who use the software you are redesigning.

> **Note:** This is only a research plan — you do not need to conduct the interviews yet. The interviews will be conducted during the next stage of the project.

> **Suggestion:** If you have time, do a practice run of your interview questions with a teammate or friend. You'll quickly discover which questions are confusing, which ones get one-word answers, and where you need better follow-ups. It's much better to find these problems now than during your actual interviews.

## Deliverable

Submit a single document (exported to PDF or DOCX) for your whole team that includes:

1. **Background research summaries** (may reuse or revise your Week 6 work)
2. **Interview script** (introduction followed by 5–10 questions)
3. **Survey questions** (5–7 questions)
4. **Research plan** describing who you intend to interview and survey, and how

## AI Expectations

AI use for this assignment is allowed in a **limited, research-support role**.

**You may use AI tools to:**
- Help brainstorm or refine search queries when looking for evidence of user problems
- Help locate potential communities, forums, or review sources where users discuss relevant issues
- Support writing clarity, organization, tone, or grammar in your final document
- Help refine wording of interview or survey questions after your team has drafted them

**AI may not be used to:**
- Conduct the background research on your behalf
- Summarize sources you did not personally review
- Invent user complaints, forum posts, or research findings
- Generate interview or survey questions without your team first designing them
- Create your research plan or determine who you should interview or survey

This assignment is intended to help your team practice identifying real user problems and preparing tools to investigate them. Your research summaries, interview questions, and survey design should reflect your team's own reasoning and investigation, even if AI is later used to refine wording.

**If AI tools are used, include a brief acknowledgment describing how they were used.**
