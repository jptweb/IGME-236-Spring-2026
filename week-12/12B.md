## Read This If You Don't Have Something to Test Yet

I feel I could stressed this harder on Tuesday (in my defense the checklist said it): **you should have something testable by today.** If you don't, that's the priority for this class period.

**Don't leave today without, at minimum:**

- Every screen drawn out and locked in 100% (paper or digital)
- A clear plan for who's building what
- Your group on the same page about the flow

**If you have time and your Figma edu accounts are set up:** start building in Figma and use collaborative editing to divide and conquer. It will take longer than you think.

**If you can't get into Figma today:** sketch every screen on paper. That counts as a lo-fi. Photograph it cleanly so it can be tested.

Either way: have something testable ASAP so you can actually run tests over the weekend then compile a nice report and form your reflections!

Other groups are sitting right next to you. Walk over, ask them to try your prototype, and offer to test theirs in return. You'll get more useful feedback in 15 minutes of cross-group testing than an hour of guessing.
